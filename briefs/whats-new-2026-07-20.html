What's New — 20 July 2026

A short weekly read of what's coming, what's landed and what matters for Gateshead's economy. Each item says why it's here. Quiet weeks will say so — and on the data side, this was one: nothing landed last week at Gateshead-level geography. The week ahead is much busier.

The week ahead — data

Tue 21 July · ONS

UK Labour Market, July 2026 — headline release, regional tables, claimant count and PAYE RTI

Why it matters: the month's core labour market read, and this one carries the local detail: June claimant counts by local authority (the direct Gateshead refresh) and PAYE Real Time Information payrolled-employee counts and pay at local authority level. It lands nine days before the first Quarterly Economy Monitor (Thu 30 July), which will build its labour market chapter on this vintage.

Wed 22 July · ONS

Consumer price inflation, June 2026

Why it matters: the deflator for every real-terms wage and income figure we publish, and a direct input to the cost-of-living product line. June's figure will be the price basis for the July monitor. National only — no local breakdown.

Wed 29 July · ONS · confirmed

Population estimates for England and Wales, mid-2025 — local authority, single year of age

Why it matters: the population base under our claimant rate, jobs density and youth-claimant calculations. This edition also revises the mid-2022 to mid-2024 back series for the updated international migration methodology, so existing denominators shift too. It lands the day before the quarterly monitor — the monitor gets a deliberate check-and-refresh step on this release rather than treating it as routine.

Late July · ONS · date unconfirmed

Business demography, quarterly — April to June 2026

Why it matters: watch item only. Previous quarterly editions have landed around 24–25 July; if it appears it is a minor refresh to the business-base context (the fuller local-authority picture waits for the annual edition in November).

Last week — policy

Thu 16 July · Commons HCLG Committee

"Revitalising High Streets" inquiry launched — written evidence closes 7 September

Why it matters: the inquiry covers council powers (rental auctions, compulsory purchase, Community Right to Buy), business rates and the forthcoming High Streets Strategy — squarely on our high streets watch theme. Our district-centre evidence (the July high streets deep dive covering Felling, Winlaton and Chopwell) is the kind of local material the committee is asking for; whether to submit written evidence is a decision to take well before the September deadline.

Mon 20 July · Westminster

Change of Prime Minister — Andy Burnham takes office today

Why it matters: a new government programme is about to be set out, with cabinet appointments expected this week and early signals already emerging (the digital ID scheme halted; fiscal direction awaiting a new Chancellor). Every live funding stream and strategy we track — Industrial Strategy, Pride in Place, devolution deepening — now carries some reprioritisation risk until the new administration's plans firm up. The August Policy Update will take stock properly.

Machinery of government · via DfE, 13 July

Skills England moves from DfE to DWP

Why it matters: skills policy and employment support are converging in one department — relevant context for the North East LSIP, adult skills funding and the Get Britain Working agenda that our labour market and NEET work feeds.

Wed 16 July · Parliament

Summer recess began — Parliament returns 1 September

Why it matters: expect a quieter announcement flow into September; the new Prime Minister's early moves are the main exception to watch.

Live deadlines

Closes Fri 31 July

Northern Growth Strategy — views window

Why it matters: final fortnight on the £120m strategy consultation that includes the proposed Newcastle–Gateshead Mayoral Development Zone. Covered in depth in the July Policy Update; flagged here as the clock runs down.

DWP call for evidence — Carer's Allowance earnings limit

Why it matters: reform of the earnings cliff-edge touches the work-and-caring margin of economic inactivity, where Gateshead's health-related inactivity evidence sits. Low burden to track; no submission implied unless commissioned.
