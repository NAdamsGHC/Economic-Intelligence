North East Creative Industries — CCIS Baseline Dashboard

DCMS creative sector business counts, employment and Gross Value Added across the seven North East Combined Authority local authorities. Companion to the CCIS Framework Data & Evidence consultation.

Gross Value Added — best-fit creative proxy

Published GVA is only available at SIC section / 2-digit level, so this is a documented proxy, not the DCMS creative definition. See note below.
Data notes & caveats. Sources: ONS UK Business Counts (businesses, 2010–2025), ONS Business Register & Employment Survey (employees, 2015–2024) on the DCMS creative-sector basis; ONS Regional GVA (balanced) by local authority, 2023 release. • The "North East" area is the Combined Authority total (the 7 LAs); selecting it and an individual LA are alternative views — do not add them together. • "All creative" is the DCMS creative-industries total and already includes every sector shown — sectors are sub-totals of it, not additions to it. • Business and employee figures are rounded by ONS (businesses to the nearest 5, employees more coarsely), so small sectors such as Crafts and Museums are imprecise and constituent-LA sums need not equal the CA total. • GVA proxy: the creative proxy sums Information & communication (SIC 58–63) and Arts, entertainment & recreation (SIC 90–93). This over-counts telecommunications, sport, gambling and recreation and under-counts advertising and design (within SIC 72–75), architecture (within SIC 71) and crafts (within manufacturing), which cannot be isolated at this granularity. Treat as indicative scale only.
Built for the NE MSA CCIS Framework Data & Evidence consultation. DCMS sector definitions per DCMS Sectors Economic Estimates Methodology; regional GVA per ONS Regional GVA (balanced) by local authority.
